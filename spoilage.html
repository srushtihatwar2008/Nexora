🔬 How Spoilage Happens?

1️ Microbes. Bacteria, fungi thrive in warm, moist places. Higher temperature = faster multiplication.

2️ Enzymes. Ripening enzymes speed up decomposition. Cold slows them down.

3️ Moisture. Too much moisture causes mold. Too little causes withering.

4️ Time. Every hour in transit increases risk. Night transport (cooler) significantly reduces it.

🌡️ Temperature Impact Guide
0-10°C (Cold/Fridge)

Best for storage. Microbes slow down. Risk: Only 5-10%. Problem: Chill damage in some crops.

10-25°C (Room Temperature)

Moderate risk. 20-30% spoilage. Good microbe control with ventilation.

25-30°C (Warm)

High risk. Microbes multiply. 30-40% spoilage likely. Need active cooling.

>30°C (Very Hot)

Severe risk. 40-60% loss in first day. Must use cold storage or transport early morning/night.

💡 Smart Storage Tips
✓ Keep It Dry

Wet stored produce rots within 2-3 days. Dry them properly before storing.

✓ Keep It Cool

Every degree cooler = several days longer shelf life. Use shade, ventilation, wet burlap.

✓ Keep It Moving

Even 1-2 days of storage increases risk. Sell quickly. Don't wait for higher prices.

✓ Keep It Ventilated

Air gaps prevent moisture buildup and mold. Never stack airtight containers.

✓ Keep It Sorted

Remove bruised/rotten items immediately. They spread infection to neighbors fast.

💰 When Is Storage Worth It?

Don't store if: Prices are already high now. Sell immediately even if storage is available.

Store if: Prices will be 20%+ higher in 1-2 weeks AND spoilage risk is low.

Example Calculation:

Current price: ₹2000/bag
Expected price after 5 days: ₹2400/bag
Storage cost: ₹50
Spoilage loss: 10% (₹200)

Net benefit: ₹2400 - ₹200 - ₹50 = ₹2150 vs ₹2000 = +₹150 profit
