📝 Example: Wheat Harvest

If you planted wheat on Oct 15, 2025, the optimal harvest time would be around March 25, 2026 (165 days later).

Why? Because wheat needs 160-180 days to mature, and March-April weather is perfect for harvest in North India - dry, cool, no rain.

💡 You can harvest anytime between March 15 - April 15, but March 25 gives you the highest yield and best price.

❓ Common Questions
Can I harvest earlier to sell at higher prices?

Early harvest means lower yield and poor quality. The grain will be soft, wet, and more prone to spoilage. Even if prices are high, losses will be more than gains.

Better strategy: Harvest at optimal time, then store smartly and sell when prices are better.

What if monsoon comes before harvest?

This is a real risk in some years. Earthworm checks the 3-month forecast and will warn you if heavy rain is expected.

If rain comes: Harvest immediately (even if slightly early) rather than lose the whole crop to rot and fungal diseases.

How accurate is the harvest date advice?

Our system checks weather forecasts, historical data, and your location. The advice is typically ±5 days accurate.

We show you the "optimal window" (10-15 days) to account for daily variations. You harvest when the crop looks ready AND the date is in the window.
